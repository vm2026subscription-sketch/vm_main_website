Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about applying for scholarships.

Who is eligible to apply for these scholarships?

Eligibility varies by scheme — merit-based (academic excellence), need-based (economically weaker sections), government (reserved categories, defence wards, regional domicile), and special schemes (women, specially-abled). Check each scholarship's individual details for exact criteria.

Can I apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously?

You can submit applications for multiple scholarships, but government guidelines generally allow availing funds from only one central or state government scholarship at a time. You may combine a government scholarship with a private/corporate scholarship depending on the private entity's terms.

What documents are commonly required?

Aadhaar Card, previous academic marksheets (Class 10, 12, or Degree semesters), Income Certificate, Caste/Category Certificate (if applicable), Domicile Certificate, bank passbook (Aadhaar-seeded), and fee receipt of the current academic year.

How is the scholarship amount disbursed?

Most central and state scholarships are disbursed via Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) into the student's Aadhaar-seeded bank account. Some institutional or corporate scholarships may disburse the tuition fee portion directly to the college.
