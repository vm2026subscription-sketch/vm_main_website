Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about applying for scholarships and securing financial aid.

1. Who is eligible to apply for these scholarships?

Eligibility varies depending on the specific scholarship scheme. Generally, scholarships are categorized into merit-based (for academic excellence), need-based (for economically weaker sections), government schemes (for reserved categories, defence wards, or regional domicile), and special schemes (for women and specially-abled students). Check the individual details of each scholarship to confirm exact criteria.

2. Can I apply for multiple scholarships simultaneously?

While you can submit applications for multiple scholarships, government guidelines usually stipulate that a student can only avail funds from one central or state government scholarship at a time. However, you may be allowed to combine a government scholarship with a private or corporate scholarship depending on the private entity's terms.

3. What common documents are required during application?

Commonly required documents include: Aadhaar Card, Previous Academic Marksheets (Class 10, 12, or Degree semesters), Income Certificate issued by a competent authority, Caste/Category Certificate (if applicable), Domicile Certificate, Bank Passbook copy (seeded with Aadhaar), and Fee Receipt of the current academic year.

4. How is the scholarship amount disbursed?

Most central and state government scholarships are disbursed through Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) directly into the student's Aadhaar-seeded bank account. Some institutional or corporate scholarships may disburse the tuition fee portion directly to the college or institute.
